NAAN BREAD

Delicious eaten warm as an idea side to a curry

Ingredients
  • 250g plain flour or atta flour
  • 1*7g sachet dried yeasr
  • 25g butter or ghee
  • 150ml natural yoghurt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1bsp nigella seeds
  • 50g caster sugar and dessicated coconut (optional)
    • Recipie

    1. Put 125ml warm water into a bowl and sprinkle over the yeast. Leave for 10-15 mins or until frothy.
    2. In a larger bowl put the flour, ½ tsp salt and baking powder
    3. Mix together and makr a well in which to pour melted butter, nigella seeds, yeast and yoghurt
    4. Start to knead until if is of a soft, not too sticky consistency
    5. Transfer to a floured surface and cover and leave in a warm place for about an hour or until doubled inside
    6. Divide the dough into about 6 balls and put them on a baking tray dusted with flour and cover with a damp tea towel
    7. Heat a large non-stick frying pan over a high heat. Take one of the balls of dough and roll it out to form a teardrop shape that’s approximately 21cm long and around 13cm at the widest part.
    8. Here you can add sugar and dessicated coconut to make sweek naan
    9. When the pan is very hot, carefully lay the naan bread into it. Let it dry fry and puff up for about 3 mins, then turn over and cook on the other side for another 3-4 mins or until cooked through and charred in patches
